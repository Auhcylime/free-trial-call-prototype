## Card 1 — Behavior (red)
**Title:** "Why does your Maine Coon follow you to the bathroom?"

**Mochi response:**
> It's actually pretty common, especially with Maine Coons. They're social cats that bond closely with their person, and a closed door feels like a mystery they need to solve. It's not anxiety in most cases, just curiosity and wanting to be near you. If it's paired with clinginess, excessive meowing, or changes in eating, it could be worth a quick check. <call>Talk to a vet about your Maine Coon's behavior</call> They can help you figure out if it's just personality or something to keep an eye on.

## Card 2 — Play (orange)
**Title:** "Best toys for a 1 year, 6 month Maine Coon – what actually works?"

**Mochi response:**
> At a year and a half, your Maine Coon still has a ton of energy and their hunting instincts are fully dialed in. Wand toys with feathers or crinkle attachments are usually the biggest hit, since they mimic prey movement. Puzzle feeders are great too, especially for a smart breed like a Maine Coon. Skip the small plastic balls and go for larger interactive toys they can wrestle with. Rotating toys every few days keeps things fresh so they don't lose interest.

**Follow-up suggestions:**
- "How much playtime does my cat need?"
- "Is my Maine Coon bored?"
- "What's the best scratching post for a big cat?"

## Card 3 — Grooming (green)
**Title:** "How often should you groom semi-long coats?"

**Mochi response:**
> For semi-long coats, aim for 2 to 3 brushing sessions per week. This keeps mats from forming, especially behind the ears, under the belly, and around the back legs. A stainless steel comb works well for detangling, and a slicker brush helps remove loose fur. During shedding season (spring and fall), you might want to bump it up to daily. <call>Ask a vet about your cat's coat care routine</call> They can also check for skin issues that sometimes hide under thicker fur.

**Follow-up suggestions:**
- "What brush is best for my cat's coat?"
- "Is my cat shedding too much?"
- "How often should I bathe my cat?"

## Card 4 — Bonding (lime)
**Title:** "How to tell if your Maine Coon actually likes you"

**Mochi response:**
> Good news: if your Maine Coon slow-blinks at you, that's basically a cat "I love you." Head bunting (pressing their forehead into you), kneading, and showing you their belly are all strong trust signals. Maine Coons also tend to follow their favorite person from room to room and "chirp" or trill when they're happy to see you. If they bring you a toy or curl up near you (even if not on your lap), that counts too. Not every cat is a lap cat, but proximity is their way of choosing you.

**Follow-up suggestions:**
- "Why does my cat knead on me?"
- "Is it normal that my cat doesn't like being held?"
- "How do I bond more with my cat?"
